01 — Context

What we are looking at

A 58-acre holding on Beulah Road, adjacent to the OLF-8 master-planning area and an active FDOT corridor study — undeveloped today, and unusually well-positioned for the decisions now in motion around it.

The parcel

The Diocese holds roughly 58 acres of contiguous, undeveloped land on Beulah Road in Escambia County. The site sits directly against the OLF-8 outlying-field disposition area, where county and state planning will define road alignment, density, and access over the next two years.

Its value is not intrinsic to the dirt; it is a function of what gets decided at its edges — chiefly the corridor that will or will not run through it.

Why now

The FDOT corridor study and the county's OLF-8 master plan are both in their formative window. Decisions made informally today harden into right-of-way and entitlement positions tomorrow.

Counterparties already at the table include Escambia County, FDOT, GMC, and Crabtree Group. The Diocese is a landowner among them — not yet a party shaping the outcome.

Four structures, weighed

Outright sale converts land to cash and ends the relationship. The other three keep the Diocese in the value it helps create — each trading upfront proceeds for control and compounding return.

Recommendation

A ground lease or PPP keeps the Diocese in the long-term value of what its land enables — and preserves the control that mission stewardship requires. Outright sale should be the fallback, not the default.

Selling land is the one decision that cannot be revisited. Where control and patience are affordable, keep the option open.

04 — Recommendation & next steps

What we recommend

Move on the corridor first, structure for control second, and let proceeds follow from a defined position — not the other way around. The sequence below follows the Virtuous Cycle Process.

I
Secure a corridor positionEngage FDOT and Escambia County to define right-of-way intent before alignment hardens. File the public-records picture into a single corridor brief.
Root · 0–60 days
II
Convene the counterpartiesBring GMC and Crabtree Group into one feasibility scope so engineering, entitlement, and finance assumptions are reconciled, not parallel.
Align · 30–90 days
III
Structure for controlDraft ground-lease and PPP term sheets in parallel; test each against proceeds, value capture, and mission control before committing.
Shape · 60–150 days
IV
Decide with leadershipBring a single recommended structure with ranges and gates to the Bishop, CFO, and COO for a clear go / hold decision.
Weave · 120–180 days
V
Steward the positionHold the corridor and entitlement gains as the asset they are; let value compound into the next phase rather than discounting it to close.
Return · ongoing

Build with, not for. A position defined with the community and its public partners is worth more — and lasts longer — than one negotiated against them.
