The approach

One process, five turns

Every engagement runs the Virtuous Cycle Process — beginning in the place, reconciling interests, and returning value to its source. For Beulah Road, the work concentrates in the first three phases this year, with the last two carried into delivery.

I
RootListen & Locate
Walk the parcel; map the counterparties already at the table — county, FDOT, GMC, Crabtree — and surface the Diocese's objectives and constraints as concrete facts.
II
AlignAlign & Assess
Reconcile mission, finance, and timing; test corridor and disposition options against the instruments that actually exist — entitlement, right-of-way, and ground-lease structures.
III
ShapeDesign & Develop
Translate alignment into a corridor position, a recommended structure, and the value-carrying decisions — framed in ranges, ready to file and to negotiate.
IV
WeaveBuild & Belong
Carry the position into the public process and the deal table, so the outcome belongs to the place and not only to the parties drawing the lines.
V
ReturnSteward & Sustain
Steward what was secured; let value compound under a structure that keeps mission control and feeds the next turn of the cycle.

We build with, not for. The Diocese stays the principal at every turn; our role is to make its hand visible at the tables where value is decided.
