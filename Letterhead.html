Re Beulah Road — transmittal of advisory report (VC-DIO-2026-07)
Dear David,

Enclosed is our advisory report on the Diocese's Beulah Road parcel and the disposition and partnership options now open to it. The work began where it should — on the land itself, and in conversation with the people whose decisions shape its edges.

The finding is plain. The parcel is not one decision but several — corridor, structure, timing, and cost-share — and the spread between a good and a poor outcome on each is measured in millions, not basis points. The street-typology and corridor commitment is the single largest lever, and it is being decided by others unless the Diocese moves first.

We recommend the Diocese lock a corridor position this year and pursue a ground-lease or public–private structure that keeps mission control while letting long-term value compound. The window for that move is real but finite; informal assurances on right-of-way do not survive a change in counterparty.

Read the land as leverage, not inventory — the decision in front of the Diocese this year sets the value it will hold for a generation.

I would welcome thirty minutes with you and the Bishop to walk the recommendation and the next two gates. I will follow up early next week to find a time.

With respect,
Jarah Jacquay
Managing Principal
