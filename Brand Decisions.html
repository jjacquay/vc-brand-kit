01
The common good, stated plainly
All three lead with purpose in concrete, un-precious language. VC already has the phrase — “land, livelihood, and the common good.” Lead with it everywhere, and drop the softer “soul/purpose” framings.
02
People and place at the center
Toole “moves people”; Fourth Economy is people-centered; Capita starts from dignity. For VC this means naming real places and people — and letting the Pensacola Bay watercolor (and photography) do the emotional work the palette won't.
03
A clear, declarative voice
Confident sentence-headlines, generous white space, ideas before ornament. VC's hero is one plainspoken sentence; restraint is the signal of command — which is already your “restraint is the brand.”
04
Forward-looking, but grounded
Resilient futures, “envision the possible,” renewal. VC frames today's decision by the value it compounds tomorrow — the virtuous cycle — pairing rigor with genuine optimism.
05
Warmth from imagery, not a second color
Capita earns warmth with one bold accent over white; you've retired off-family secondaries. So VC's warmth comes through the watercolor's own sunset tones and real photography — the olive family + neutrals stay the only brand colors, and the work supplies the heat.
